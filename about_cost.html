About Corgis / Cost

Corgis, like many other dogs, can be more expensive than one would expect. Take a look at the costs and really consider if you can afford a corgi before deciding to adopt one!

Cost of Owning a Corgi

In the scenarios listed below, we are comparing an average corgi who lives to be 14 years old with moderate health issues, compared to a high-maintenance and low-maintenance corgi.

Time Cost of Owning a Corgi

  • Energy level varies widely between individual Corgis. All will need regular walks and some playtime and training to be happy. Many Corgis need much more than that. Some are almost impossible to tire out, and young Corgis (under 2) may never stop moving. Some can go for a two-hour off-leash hike, take a thirty-minute power nap, and start dropping tennis balls to play. Corgis like this need to learn some sort of “settle” or “enough” command or you won’t have any peace, and quite a few Corgis need a job (agility, herding, competition-level obedience) to be happy. A Corgi with unspent energy very difficult to live with because they can be bossy and also have a tendency to bark.
  • Bottom Line: Expect to walk your corgi twice a day for at least 45 minutes (or a good 30 minute run).

Preparing for Health Costs

  • Pupppy Insurance - $50/month
  • Corgi Insurance - $100/month
  • Hip Dysplasia - $2000
  • Degenerative Myelopathy - $2000
  • Progressive Retinal Atrophy - $1500
  • Intervertebral Disc Disease - $500
